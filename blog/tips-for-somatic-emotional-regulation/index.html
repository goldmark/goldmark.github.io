11 tips for Somatic Emotional Regulation (Body-Based Tools)

Learn 11 body-based tools to calm your nervous system, release intense emotions, and match each technique to what you feel in the moment.

emotional regulation emotional awareness tool for emotional regulation
  1. Breathing regulation:
  • Slow exhale breathing (longer exhale than inhale). Start with inhale on 4 exhale on 5, move to inhale on 5 and exhale on 6, after few breathes, inhale on 6 and exhale on 8
  1. Temperature Regulation Temperature strongly influences the vagus nerve.

  • Holding an ice pack or cold can to the face or chest

  • Splashing cool water on the face

  • Holding a warm mug or wrapping in a blanket

üëâ Helps shift out of high arousal (anger, panic) or low arousal (numbness).

  1. Deep Pressure / Containment Firm pressure signals safety to the nervous system.

  • Self-hug (arms crossed, gentle squeeze)

  • Weighted blanket or heavy jacket

  • Pressing feet firmly into the floor or back into a chair

Often helpful for anxiety, overwhelm, and grief.

  1. Scent Regulation Smell has a direct pathway to the limbic system.

  • Essential oils (lavender, citrus, eucalyptus)

  • Familiar or comforting scents

  • Fresh air

Helpful for rapid emotional shifts.

  1. Hand-to-Heart or Hand-to-Belly Contact Gentle hand placement activates calming circuits.

  • One hand on chest, one on abdomen

  • Notice warmth and pressure

This is especially effective for sadness and self-soothing.

  1. Stillness as Regulation Intentional stillness is different from freezing.

  • Sitting quietly with awareness of physical contact points

  • Noticing weight, temperature, gravity

This restores nervous system balance when done intentionally.
