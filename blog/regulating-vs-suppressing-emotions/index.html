Regulating vs suppressing emotions

Emotional regulation allows release of negative emotions. When emotions are regulated, the person can move on and respond to life events without reacting. When the person is not able to release the emotions, often stress causes muscle tension or more serious medical issues.

emotional regulation emotional awareness why tools for emotional regulation might not work supression

As a parent of three, a teacher, spouse and daughter, I often find myself in the midst of strong emotions. Trying to navigate those emotions led me to study emotional regulation vs suppression pretty extensively. I will share with you what I learned over the course of the next few weeks.

When we feel angry or disappointed but instead of yelling and screaming, we can hold our composure, are we regulating or suppressing strong unpleasant emotions? It depends on what happens internally. Emotional regulation is healthy management by acknowledging and understanding feelings to respond thoughtfully. It is the ability to notice, understand, and manage emotions in ways that are healthy, intentional, and aligned with your values. In contrast, emotional suppression is ignoring, minimizing, or pushing the feeling away to appear calm.
