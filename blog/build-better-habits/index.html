5 Simple Steps to Build Better Habits

Learn how to create lasting change through small, intentional habits that stick. Discover the science-backed approach to habit formation.

habits personal growth wellness

Why Habits Matter

Habits shape our daily lives more than we realize. Research shows that about 40% of our daily actions are habitual rather than conscious decisions. Bringing awareness of what conscious and subconscious actions we do routines can help understand how our habits control our life. You can expand or replace some of those habits. Evaluating how your current habits help you to resolve the problems you face will be a key point to creating a better life. Your current habits are just the methods you learned to use so far to accomplish daily routines and cope with life. One thing is for sure: if you really want to change a habit, make it undesirable and unattractive. Building better habits doesn’t have to be overwhelming. In my years of coaching clients through life transitions, and research into behavior science, I’ve found that sustainable change comes from small, intentional steps rather than dramatic overhauls. When we improve our habits, we improve our lives.

The 5 Steps

1. Start Small

Don’t try to change everything at once. Pick one habit and make it so easy you can’t say no. Want to read more? Put a book by your night stand. Start with one page. Make sure you read before you fall asleep. Want to exercise? Start with a five minute stretch as soon as you wake up in the morning.

2. Stack Your Habits

Link your new habit to an existing one. “After I pour my morning coffee, I will write three things I’m grateful for.” This technique, called habit stacking, makes new behaviors more likely to stick.

3. Design Your Environment

Make good habits obvious and bad habits invisible. Want to drink more water? Put a water bottle on your desk. Want to reduce screen time? Charge your phone in another room. Want to eat healthy, make fruits and vegetables easily available and store them at the front of your refrigerator. Put the less desirable foods at the back of the fridge and not easily accessible.

4. Track Your Progress

Keep it simple—a checkmark on a calendar works wonders. Seeing your streak builds momentum and motivation.

5. Be Patient and Compassionate

Habits take time to form. Research suggests anywhere from 18 to 254 days, with an average of 66 days. When you slip up (and you will), treat yourself with the same kindness you’d show a friend.

Moving Forward

Remember, you’re not aiming for perfection—you’re aiming for progress. Each small step forward is a victory worth celebrating.
